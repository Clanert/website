The Pollution Problems We Tackle

Air Quality

Tanzania's urban areas face increasing concentrations of PM2.5, NOx, SOx, and ozone, driven by industrialization and poor waste burning practices.

  • PM2.5 levels exceed WHO thresholds
  • Lack of localized monitoring stations
  • Low public awareness and enforcement

Acoustic Pollution

Noise and vibration in urban and construction zones are increasing, leading to hearing loss, stress, and reduced quality of life.

  • Transport and construction exceed safe thresholds
  • No comprehensive noise maps
  • Overlooked in EIAs

Why Air Quality Matters

Air pollution is a silent killer, affecting both developed and developing countries. In Tanzania, major urban centers face significant pollution threats from vehicles, waste, and dust.

  1. Health Impact (Global)

    According to WHO, air pollution causes 7 million premature deaths annually worldwide.

  2. Urban Air Crisis

    Dar es Salaam frequently exceeds WHO's safe air limits by 500%, due to transport, industry and open burning.

  3. Lack of Monitoring

    Tanzania lacks a national network of real-time air quality monitoring stations, making it difficult to respond effectively to pollution threats.

  4. Climate & Policy Link

    Air pollution also contributes to global warming and undermines Tanzania's Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs).

Source: WHO Global Air Quality Report 2022
